When Should You See a Lung Doctor?

Mild coughs often go away, but certain symptoms shouldn’t be ignored:

  • Chronic cough or dry cough lasting beyond 2–3 weeks
  • Persistent cough with phlegm or blood-tinged sputum
  • Asthma symptoms and wheezing (especially at night or early mornings)
  • Breathing difficulty diseases like shortness of breath during normal activity
  • Chest congestion or chest pain while breathing
  • Repeated upper or lower respiratory tract infections (URTI / LRTI)
  • Fatigue or difficulty inhaling and exhaling without clear cause

Early evaluation helps identify underlying conditions such as bronchial asthma, interstitial lung disease (ILD) or chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD) before they worsen.
