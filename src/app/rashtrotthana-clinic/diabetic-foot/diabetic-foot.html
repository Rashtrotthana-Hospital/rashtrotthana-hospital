Understanding Diabetic Foot Problems
Diabetes can silently damage the nerves and blood vessels in your feet, leading to numbness, slow-healing wounds and infections that may go unnoticed until they become serious.
Spotting the symptoms early can prevent complications and even save your limb.
Watch out for:
  • Numbness or tingling in the feet
  • Non-healing wounds or ulcers
  • Skin discoloration or swelling
  • Changes in foot shape or sensation
  • Repeated infections or pain
If you notice any of these warning signs, visit our Diabetic Foot Care Clinic for a thorough evaluation and personalized treatment plan.

    Signs You Shouldn’t Ignore
    Many diabetic foot complications start small but can progress rapidly if neglected.
    Consult a specialist immediately if you experience:
    • Persistent numbness or tingling
    • Ulcers or wounds that don’t heal
    • Foot swelling or color changes
    • Repeated infections
    • Pain, deformity or difficulty walking
    Early diagnosis and expert care can prevent infection, save your limb and improve your quality of life.
