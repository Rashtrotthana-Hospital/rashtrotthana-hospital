Symptoms
Common Diabetic Foot Symptoms include:
  • Non-healing wounds or ulcers
  • Numbness or loss of sensation
  • Foot pain or burning sensation
  • Swelling, redness, or warmth
  • Skin discoloration or thickened nails
Ignoring foot symptoms in diabetes can lead to serious complications. Early evaluation helps prevent infections and amputations.
Diabetic Foot Conditions We Treat
Common Diabetic Foot Conditions include:
  • Diabetic Foot Ulcers: Slow-healing wounds due to poor circulation
  • Peripheral Neuropathy: Nerve damage causing numbness or pain
  • Foot Infections: Bacterial or fungal infections
  • Calluses & Corns: Pressure-related skin thickening
  • Gangrene Risk: Tissue damage due to poor blood supply
Our team provides timely treatment and education to prevent progression and recurrence.
