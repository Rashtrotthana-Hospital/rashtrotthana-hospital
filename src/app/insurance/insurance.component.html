Pre-Authorization Procedure

In the case of a planned admission, you would have first consulted a doctor, who in turn would have advised you on the probable date of hospitalization. In such a case, you must apply for approval of the estimated hospital expenses directly from your TPA at least 4-5 days prior to the date of hospitalization. In case you have not applied for pre-authorization sufficiently in advance or if the doctor treating you advises you to get hospitalized immediately after the consultation, our Insurance Help Desk (Phone: 080-69239999) will assist you through the pre-authorization procedure. Normal working hours are from 8:00 a.m. to 8:00 p.m., Monday through Saturday and on Sundays and hospital holidays, from 9:00 a.m. to 5:00 p.m.

The Pre-Authorization Procedure is Detailed Below:
  1. Establish contact with the Insurance Help Desk at the hospital, or please refer here for all the information related to cashless processing or reimbursement.
  2. Collect the pre-authorization forms pertaining to your TPA, TPA checklist and TPA undertaking form from our Insurance Help desk or download all these forms. Please present your health insurance card for the staff to assist you appropriately.
  3. Form Filling: Your pre-authorization form will have two sections:
  4. General details on the health insurance policy are to be filled in by you (the Insurance Help Desk will assist you in case you have any difficulty).
  5. The treatment recommended for you is to be filled out and duly signed by the doctor who is treating you (do not attempt to fill out this section; contact the TPA Help Desk in case of any difficulty). The TPA checklist and TPA undertaking forms are self-explanatory.
  6. Return the completed form (filled out and signed) along with copies of the documents mentioned in the TPA check list to the Insurance Help desk to make a single pdf file of all your documents (as per the TPA checklist) and email to info@rashtrotthanahospital.com, with subject as 'Patient's name, Patient Registration Number and mobile number'. The personnel at the desk will verify the form for its completeness and let you know in case of any discrepancy.
  7. Once the form is filled out, the Insurance Help Desk will upload the documents to the portal and transmit them to the office of your TPA.
  8. The Insurance Help Desk will revert to you with the approval or denial status and the status of approval is updated on your registered mobile number.
  9. At the time of admission, you shall be required to pay a differential amount of admission deposit and based on the TPA approval. The differential amount would be refunded on discharge after receiving final approval as per the final bill. You shall, then, be refunded the deposit amount with deductions if any.

You shall be required to
  1. Settle the difference, if any, if your bill exceeds the designated insurance amount.
  2. Pay for all non-medical expenses, as your policy will not cover them.
  3. Check all the bills and prescriptions for any medicine that is billed but not administered to you.
  4. Note the total amount of the bill for your records for future purposes.
  5. Submit all the medical documents, including the lab reports, claim forms, discharge summary and final bill, to the hospital before being discharged.
The hospital then submits all necessary documents to TPA. TPA processes the bill based on eligibility and actual cost. You shall, then, be refunded the deposit amount with deductions if any.
What if the cost of treatment exceeds the approved sum?
Generally, the TPA only approves a part of the expenses of the treatment and only after the hospital sends the final bill along with the discharge summary and other reports does it approve the entire amount. Sometimes the hospitals will request that the TPA increase the amount approved if needed during the treatment.
Q 1. Which Documents Are Required To Process Reimbursement?

A. Part A & Part B Forms Of The Respective Insurance Company

B. All Original Investigation Reports

C. Original Bills

D. Indoor Case Paper (If Required)

For Queries, You May Call 080-69239999 From 8 A.M. To 8 P.M.

Q 2. Where Is Part 'B' Form Available?

Please Check Your Insurance Company's Website Or Contact Your Insurance Agent.

Q 3. Part B Form Is Filled Out By Whom?

Part 'B' Is Filled Out By The IP Billing Or Insurance Coordinator After The Patient's Discharge.

Q 4. Where Is The Indoor Case Paper Request Form Available?

It Is Available In The Insurance And Billing Department And On The Hospital Website.

Q 5. Which Documents Are Required For The Indoor Case Paper Request?

Patient's Signed Consent Letter Or Indoor Case Paper Request Form With A Copy Of Their Pan Card, Aadhaar Card, Or Driving License.

Q 6. When Shall I Receive All Indoor Case Papers?

You Can Receive Your Indoor Case Papers From The Medical Record Department Within 3 Working Days From The Date Of Application.

In an emergency hospitalization, the important thing is to get the patient treatment as soon as possible. The Insurance Help Desk will take up your case on a fast-track basis with your TPA and is likely to receive approvals within 8 hours during any working day.

Step 1: Show your health insurance card and fill out the pre-authorization form.

Step 2: The Insurance desk in the hospital will fast-track the process for your cashless process, but in case you cannot wait for approval, you can pay the deposit if demanded by the hospital, start the treatment and reimburse the expense later.

Step 3: Generally, the time taken to process an emergency case is 8 hours, but it will depend on the insurance company or TPA. You need to follow up with the Insurance help desk to know the status of the request.
