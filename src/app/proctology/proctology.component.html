What is Proctology?

Proctology, a highly specialized area of medicine also known as colorectal surgery, is a medical specialty focused on diagnosing and treating disorders of the rectum, anus and colon.

What is Proctology?

Colorectal surgeons treat intestinal tract disorders. This tract consists of the colon, rectum, anal canal and perianal area. The intestinal tract's conditions include:

  • Abscesses and fistulae: These are infections near the anus and rectum
  • Anal skin tags: These are small skin bumps around the anus.
  • Colon and rectal cancer: This cancer begins in the colon or rectum.
  • Diverticulitis: This is a disease that causes pouches to form in weak spots along the digestive tract.
  • Fissures: These are small tears in the anal lining.
  • Hemorrhoids: These are swollen veins that can form internally or externally around the anus.
  • Inflammatory bowel disease (IBD): IBD is a group of inflammatory disorders that includes ulcerative colitis and Crohnâ€™s disease.
  • Irritable bowel syndrome (IBS): IBS is a group of intestinal symptoms unrelated to IBD that can include bloating, cramping, constipation and diarrhea.
  • Polyps: These are precancerous growths that have the potential to become colorectal cancer.
  • Rectal prolapse: This is a condition in which the rectum begins to fall from its position into the anal opening.

Additionally, colorectal surgeons can treat sexually transmitted infections (STIs), including:

  • Chlamydia
  • Genital herpes
  • Gonorrhea
  • Syphilis

When to See a Proctologist:

You should see a proctologist if you have diseases concerning:

  • Persistent rectal pain or bleeding
  • Anal itching or burning
  • Changes in bowel habits, such as chronic constipation or diarrhea
  • Unexplained weight loss or fatigue
  • Lumps or swelling around the anus
  • Severe abdominal pain or cramping

Diagnostic and Treatment Options:

Colorectal surgeons are trained to perform a variety of diagnostic tests and surgical procedures:

  • Anoscopy: This procedure helps identify abnormalities in the anus and rectum.
  • Colonoscopy: During a colonoscopy, the doctor examines the entire colon and screens for colorectal cancer. They may also remove polyps
  • Digital rectal exams: During this physical examination, the doctor uses their fingers to examine the lower rectum and prostate.
  • Endorectal ultrasound: This is an imaging test to help diagnose colorectal cancer.
  • Proctoscopy: In this procedure, the doctor examines the rectum and anus, takes tissue for biopsy or removes polyps.
  • Sigmoidoscopy: The doctor examines the sigmoid colon, the lower third of your colon, during this procedure.
